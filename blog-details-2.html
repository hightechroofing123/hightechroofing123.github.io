GRP Roofing: The Durable Solution Your Property Needs

  • Patrick Moore
  • Sept 2, 2023

Looking for a roofing solution that can withstand the test of time and the challenges of South West England's climate? This blog post is all about GRP roofing, a tried-and-true choice for those seeking durability and longevity. Glass Reinforced Plastic (GRP) roofing has a remarkable track record, with some installations lasting up to 30 years when done correctly.

GRP roofing, also known as fiberglass roofing, is celebrated for its exceptional durability and effectiveness. Here's why it's the go-to choice for residential and commercial properties in Devon and Cornwall:

1. Unparalleled Durability

GRP roofing is incredibly robust and resistant to the elements. It can withstand heavy rain, strong winds, and even the corrosive effects of saltwater if your property is near the coast.


2. Longevity

When installed correctly by experienced professionals, GRP roofs can last for decades without showing signs of wear. This longevity means fewer worries about roof replacements and repairs.


3. Seamless and Waterproof
GRP roofing is applied as a liquid, creating a seamless and waterproof barrier on your flat roof. This eliminates the potential for leaks and provides reliable protection year-round.


4. Versatility

GRP roofing is suitable for a wide range of applications, including residential homes, commercial buildings, garages, balconies, and more. Its versatility makes it an excellent choice for various property types.


5. Low Maintenance

Once installed, GRP roofing requires minimal maintenance. Occasional cleaning is typically all that's needed to keep it looking and performing at its best.


6. Customizable

GRP roofing allows for customization in terms of color and finish. You can choose an option that complements the aesthetic of your property.


7. Environmentally Friendly

GRP roofing is known for its sustainability and minimal environmental impact. Its long lifespan reduces the need for frequent replacements, which can generate waste.


8. Cost-Effective

While the initial investment in a GRP roof may be slightly higher than some alternatives, its durability and longevity make it a cost-effective choice in the long run.
